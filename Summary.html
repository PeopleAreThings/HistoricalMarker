Ice cream soda or Ice cream floats are a delicious treat made here in philadelphia. With its small beginnings as a recipe substitute, the soda spread all across the nation, & the world. Nowadays people all over the world enjoy ice cream floats and put their own twists on the now classic beverage. While reading you will discover the history of ice cream and its popularity today. Welcome to the world of ice cream floats.

Ice cream floats were invented by one man, so let's talk about him. The man in question is Robert M. Green. He was a drugstore clerk who was hired by the Franklin Institute in 1874 to dispense soda. Nowadays, he is well known in the Ice cream float industry, and little known in the general public. Now that we know the inventor let's go on to the invention.

The story of ice cream floats is an interesting one. As the common story goes Green was serving soda during a hot day at the 25th anniversary of the Franklin Institute. While serving, disaster struck as green ran out of ice, so he improvised. Combining a mix of ice cream and soda he made the first ice cream float. This is an interesting story however according to Green the answer is different.

Green's story starts much the same way. On a hot day in the Franklin Institute green was serving ice cream. However, the story becomes different from here. According to Green he made the sweet treat to attract more customers away from a fancier soda machine. Now that we know the story let's finish up by going over what ice cream soda actually is.

If you wish to create this classic treat, follow along closely. First get sweet cream, syrup and carbonated water. Next, put them together in a bowl. Finally, mix it together & serve. Hope you enjoyed this deep dive into the carbonated waters of ice cream soda.
